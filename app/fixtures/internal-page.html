The rock act became the first band to perform a major concert at Olympic Park Stadium, and Slash and Duff McKagan were in the audience to watch as Axl performed with his new band after stepping in for Brian Johnson, who is suffering hearing issues.

The gig marked Rose's first AC/DC show without the aid of a throne he has been seated in since early April (16) after breaking his foot during a surprise Guns N' Roses club show in Los Angeles.

Slash and Duff were suitably impressed, describing their bandmate's London show as "an epic experience" on Twitter, with bassist McKagan adding, "ACDC/Axl gig at Olympic Stadium was nothing short of awesome last night! Memorable evening to say the least."

The singer let the crowd know Britain's recent cold snap was never going to keep him from the stage, telling fans, "They told me it's cold. It's not f**king cold, it's actually kind of nice. It's a nice place to be."

He was in classic form throughout the concert, even poking fun at his AC/DC bandmates, joking, "Now you know what (The Queen's London base) Buckingham Palace guards do in their free time."

AC/DC has three shows remaining on the European leg of their Rock or Bust tour before Axl heads back to the U.S. to prepare for Guns N' Roses shows and more AC/DC gigs in North America throughout the summer.
